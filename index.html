Hi there! I'm Bryan, an incoming second-year student at UC Berkeley (go bears!).

By day, I prime into my left-brain drive for logic & critical thinking through studying Materials Science/EECS, tinkering with Engineering Design at the Jacobs Makerspace, and writing analytically as an English minor.

By night, I immerse in my right-brain passions for web design & video production, write blogs, and develop an aesthetic-like appreciation for Mathematics.

Some fun facts: I'm 5' 8" tall (5' 9" on a good day), and in my free time I enjoy playing Tetris, getting thick as a brick at the RSF, practicing taiko drums at Savio Steps, listening to Dissect Podcast, fanboying over the Ace Attorney series, and reading a good book.

    Research
    Aug 2020 -
    Present

    Bediako Lab

    I will begin my undergraduate research career in the College of Chemistry under the guidance of grad student Jonathon Nessralla and Professor Kwabena Bediako. My research will focus on ionic diffusion phenomena in electronic materials that can be purposed for neuromorphic computing: a bio-inspired paradigm for computer architecture that can support computations with neuron-like speeds much faster than those of current Von Neumann architecture.

    The applications of faster computing speeds through neuromorphic architecture are limitless. One that personally appeal to me is the potential for enhanced machine learning methods to handle large-volume data from complex climate change models.
