    Research
    Aug 2020 -
    Present

    Bediako Lab

    I will begin my undergraduate research career remotely with the Bediako lab in the College of Chemistry. My research will focus on ionic diffusion phenomena in electronic materials that can be purposed for neuromorphic computing: a bio-inspired paradigm for computer architecture that can support computations with neuron-like speeds much faster than those of current Von Neumann architecture.

    The applications of faster computing speeds through neuromorphic architecture are limitless. One that personally appeals to me is the potential for enhanced machine learning methods to handle large-volume data from complex climate change models.
